Nag Panchami Festival

A Celebration of Serpents and Divinity

About Nag Panchami

Nag Panchami is a legendary Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of Nagas, or serpents. Observed across India, it is celebrated on the fifth day of the bright half of the lunar month of Shravana (July/August). This festival is not just about reverence for serpents but also about embracing the mysteries of nature and the divine connection between humans and the natural world.

Ancient Traditions, Timeless Devotion

Significance

In Hindu mythology, serpents hold a powerful place, often depicted as protectors of treasures and symbols of rebirth, immortality, and wisdom. Nag Panchami is a day to honor these mystical beings, believed to be close to the gods. It is said that worshipping Nagas brings protection and blessings, safeguarding devotees from snake bites and other dangers.

How It is Celebrated

On this sacred day, devotees clean their homes, draw intricate snake patterns at their doorsteps, and offer milk, sweets, and flowers to snake idols. In temples, priests perform special pujas, chanting mantras to appease the Nagas. The festival is marked by a deep sense of reverence and a connection to the ancient traditions that have been passed down through generations.
